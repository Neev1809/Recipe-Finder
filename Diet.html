Grilled Paneer Salad

Description

A healthy and flavorful salad made with soft grilled paneer and fresh vegetables, tossed in a light and zesty dressing. Packed with protein and nutrients, it’s perfect for a light meal or a diet-friendly snack.

Ingredients

  • 200g paneer (cubed)
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 cucumber (sliced)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1/2 onion (sliced)
  • Lettuce leaves or mixed greens
  • 1/2 capsicum (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp honey (optional)
  • Salt & pepper (to taste)

Instructions

  1. Toss paneer cubes with olive oil, salt, pepper, and chili flakes
  2. Grill on a pan or tawa until lightly golden on all sides
  3. In a large bowl, combine cucumber, tomato, onion, capsicum, and lettuce
  4. Mix lemon juice, olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper in a small bowl
  5. Add grilled paneer to the vegetables
  6. Pour the dressing over and toss gently
  7. Serve immediately for best taste and texture

Tips

  • Use fresh paneer for best texture
  • Don’t overcook paneer—it becomes chewy
  • Add boiled corn or olives for extra flavor
  • Skip honey for a fully low-calorie version

Oats Vegetable Upma

Description

A healthy twist on the classic upma, made with nutritious oats and fresh vegetables. Light, filling, and packed with fiber, this dish is perfect for a wholesome breakfast or a quick diet-friendly meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup oats (rolled or quick oats)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1–2 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, peas, beans)
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Dry roast oats in a pan for 3–4 minutes until slightly crisp
  2. Keep aside
  3. Heat oil in a pan
  4. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds; let them splutter
  5. Add chopped onions and green chilies, sauté until soft
  6. Add mixed vegetables and cook for 3–4 minutes
  7. Add turmeric and salt
  8. Pour water and bring it to a boil
  9. Add roasted oats and mix well
  10. Cook on low heat until oats absorb water and become soft
  11. Add lemon juice and garnish with coriander leaves
  12. Serve hot

Tips

  • Use rolled oats for better texture
  • Adjust water for softer or drier consistency
  • Add peanuts for crunch and protein
  • Avoid overcooking to keep it light and fluffy

Stir-Fried Vegetables

Description

A quick, healthy, and colorful dish made with fresh vegetables lightly sautéed in minimal oil. Packed with nutrients and flavor, this recipe is perfect for a light meal or a diet-friendly side.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup carrot (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup capsicum (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup beans (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup baby corn (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp oil (olive oil preferred)
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan or wok on high heat
  2. Add chopped garlic and sauté for a few seconds
  3. Add all the vegetables and stir-fry on high heat for 4–5 minutes
  4. Keep stirring to maintain crunch and color
  5. Add soy sauce, salt, pepper, and chili flakes
  6. Mix well so all vegetables are evenly coated
  7. Cook until vegetables are slightly tender but still crunchy
  8. Serve hot immediately

Tips

  • Don’t overcook—keep veggies slightly crunchy
  • Use high heat for best texture
  • Add tofu or paneer for extra protein
  • Avoid too much sauce to keep it healthy

Sprouts Salad

Description

A fresh, nutritious, and protein-rich salad made with sprouted legumes, crunchy vegetables, and a light tangy dressing. Perfect for a healthy snack or diet-friendly meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup mixed sprouts (moong, chana)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1 cucumber (chopped)
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped, optional)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp chaat masala

Instructions

  1. Wash and drain the sprouts properly
  2. (Optional) Lightly boil or steam the sprouts for 3–5 minutes if you prefer them soft
  3. In a bowl, add sprouts, onion, tomato, cucumber, and green chili
  4. Add salt, pepper, and chaat masala
  5. Pour lemon juice and mix well
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves
  7. Serve immediately

Tips

  • Use fresh sprouts for best taste
  • Don’t add salt too early—it can release water
  • Add boiled corn or pomegranate for extra flavor
