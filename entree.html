Chole


Description

A hearty North Indian curry made with chickpeas simmered in a rich, spiced tomato gravy. Bold, tangy flavors make it a comforting and satisfying meal, best enjoyed with rice or bhature.

Ingredients

  • Chickpeas (soaked overnight or canned)
  • Onions (finely chopped)
  • Tomatoes (pureed or chopped)
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Green chilies
  • Oil or ghee
  • Spices: cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, chole masala
  • Salt
  • Fresh coriander (for garnish)
  • Optional: tea bag (for darker color), amchur (dry mango powder)


Instructions

  1. Cook the chickpeas until soft (pressure cook soaked ones or use canned), then set aside while you heat oil in a pan and sauté cumin seeds, finely chopped onions, and ginger-garlic paste until the onions turn golden and aromatic.
  2. Add tomatoes along with all the spices and cook thoroughly until the mixture thickens and oil begins to separate, then add the cooked chickpeas with some water and let it simmer so the gravy deepens in flavor and coats the chickpeas well.
  3. Finish with garam masala and fresh coriander, letting it rest briefly before serving so the flavors settle.


Tips

  • Let it rest for 15–20 minutes after cooking; the flavor deepens a lot.
  • Slightly mash a few chickpeas while simmering for a thicker gravy.
  • A tea bag while boiling chickpeas gives that authentic dark color.

Palak Paneer


Description

A wholesome dish featuring soft paneer cubes in a smooth, vibrant spinach gravy. Lightly spiced and packed with nutrients, it’s a perfect balance of health and taste.

Ingredients

  • Fresh spinach (palak)
  • Paneer (cubed)
  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Green chilies
  • Oil or ghee
  • Spices: cumin seeds, turmeric, garam masala, red chili powder
  • Salt
  • Cream (optional, for richness)

Instructions

  1. Blanch the spinach in hot water, transfer it to cold water to retain color, then blend into a smooth puree while you prepare the base by sautéing cumin seeds, onions, ginger-garlic paste, and tomatoes in oil until soft and well-cooked.
  2. Add spices and cook the mixture until aromatic, then pour in the spinach puree and let it simmer gently to develop flavor without overcooking the greens.
  3. Add paneer cubes and cook for a few minutes until soft, finishing with a touch of cream if desired for extra richness.

Tips

  • Don’t overcook spinach after blending—it dulls the color and taste.
  • Soak paneer in warm water before adding to keep it soft.
  • Add a pinch of sugar or cream to balance the slight bitterness.

Veg Biryani


Description

A fragrant rice dish layered with fresh vegetables, aromatic spices, and herbs. Every bite is rich, flavorful, and beautifully spiced, making it a complete meal on its own.

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice
  • Mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, peas, potato, etc.)
  • Onion (sliced)
  • Tomato
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Green chilies
  • Yogurt (curd)
  • Oil or ghee
  • Whole spices: bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom
  • Spice powders: turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala or biryani masala
  • Fresh herbs: mint and coriander
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Soak basmati rice and cook it until about 70% done, while in another pan you heat oil or ghee and sauté whole spices, sliced onions, ginger-garlic paste, and mixed vegetables until slightly tender and fragrant.
  2. Add yogurt and powdered spices, cooking the mixture until it thickens and coats the vegetables well, creating a rich base for layering.
  3. Layer the partially cooked rice over the vegetable mixture, top with fresh mint and coriander, cover, and cook on low heat (dum) so the flavors infuse and the rice finishes cooking perfectly.

Tips

  • Always use partially cooked rice before layering—never fully cooked.
  • Fried onions (birista) on top take it to another level.
  • Seal the lid (with dough or tight cover) during dum for better aroma.
